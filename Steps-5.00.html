Case 1: Maximizing sales impact

Positive Insight:

Walmart stores saw a substantial increase in holiday sales compared to non-holiday periods, with an average rise of 19.19%. This surge, with holiday sales averaging $1.21 million versus non-holiday sales at $1.03 million, reflects Walmart's effective strategies in maximizing sales during festive seasons.


Negative Insight:

Walmart stores experienced a decrease in the Consumer Price Index (CPI), which measures the change in prices paid by consumers for goods and services, indicating a challenging situation. On average, the CPI declined by -0.38%, with CPI levels averaging 171 during holiday periods compared to 172 during non-holiday periods. This decrease signals potential pricing challenges or economic factors impacting consumer spending behavior within Walmart stores during these years.

  • We can see holiday sales compared to CPI and that CPI does not get affected.


ROI assessment Case 1:

Enhancing holiday marketing to aim for a 5% sales increase, building on the observed 19.19% uplift, could yield around $60,615 in additional revenue. Using an average weekly holiday sales baseline of $1,212,315, and factoring in estimated costs of $20,000 for the campaign and operational enhancements, the net gain amounts to approximately $40,615. This indicates a strong ROI from holiday sales initiatives.
